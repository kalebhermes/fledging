# App bar

The App Bar (or Navigation Header) is a top-anchored component that helps establish the context of the current screen and allows users to navigate through different flows. It serves as a navigation aid and provides access to key actions relevant to the screen.

A navigation bar typically appears at the top of a window or screen, helping users move through a hierarchy of content. It contains navigational elements, context, and additional options based on the screen, enabling users to orient themselves within the app.

## Accessibility

Keyboard:
- Tab: Navigate between actions
- Space or Enter: Trigger the action

## Usage

**When to use:** Use a top app bar to provide content and actions related to a current screen, such as navigation, screen headlines, and actions.
The information and actions offered are primarily contextual and specific to a screen, but may also include access to key cross-app journeys.

**Do:**
- Use the default heights to maintain optimal legibility of on-screen elements.
- Keep the app bar limited to only essential actions.
- Restrict the title and subtitle to a single line for clarity.
- Enclose the app bar in a container where appropriate based on the use case.

**Don't:**
- Reduce the app bar height below the default by applying density, as it may impact readability.
- Overpopulate it with too many actions, as it can create clutter and reduce usability.
- Use multiple lines for titles and subtitles, as it can visually overwhelm the interface.
- Leave the app bar without structure, allowing content to scroll behind it, which can cause readability and usability issues.
